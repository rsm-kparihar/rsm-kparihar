## From support tickets to marketing signals

```{python}
signals = pd.DataFrame({
    "Signal": ["Purchase intent", "Churn risk", "Product confusion", "Retention moment", "Campaign feedback"],
    "Marketing value": [88, 82, 76, 72, 66]
})

fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(8, 4.8))
ax.barh(signals["Signal"], signals["Marketing value"], color=pink)
ax.set_xlim(0, 100)
ax.set_xlabel("Illustrative marketing value")
ax.set_title("What support conversations can reveal", color=deep_pink, weight="bold")
ax.invert_yaxis()
ax.spines[["top", "right", "left"]].set_visible(False)
ax.grid(axis="x", alpha=0.18)
ax.tick_params(axis="y", length=0)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()
```

::: {.small-note}
Values are illustrative, used to show relative marketing usefulness rather than measured performance.
:::

::: {.callout}
**Marketing shift:** Support data helps teams see intent, pain points, and retention risk before they show up in dashboards.
:::

## A simple customer journey

```{python}
journey = pd.DataFrame({
    "Stage": ["Question", "AI answer", "Action", "Human help", "Loyalty"],
    "Experience score": [42, 62, 74, 84, 91]
})

fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(8, 4.8))
ax.plot(journey["Stage"], journey["Experience score"], marker="o", linewidth=3, color=deep_pink)
ax.fill_between(journey["Stage"], journey["Experience score"], [35] * len(journey), color=soft_pink, alpha=0.55)
ax.set_ylim(35, 100)
ax.set_ylabel("Illustrative customer experience score")
ax.set_title("Better conversations can improve the customer journey", color=deep_pink, weight="bold")
ax.spines[["top", "right"]].set_visible(False)
ax.grid(axis="y", alpha=0.18)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()
```
